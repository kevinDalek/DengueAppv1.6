What is Dengue?

Dengue fever is a disease caused by a family of viruses that are transmitted by mosquitoes. It is an acute illness of sudden onset that usually follows a benign course with headache, fever, exhaustion, severe joint and muscle pain, swollen glands , and rash. The presence (the ‘dengue triad’) of fever, rash, and headache (and other pains) is particularly characteristic of dengue.

Victims of dengue may have contortions due to the intense joint and muscle pain, hence the name breakbone fever.

Cause

Dengue fever can be caused by any one of four types of dengue virus: DEN-1, DEN-2, DEN-3, and DEN-4.

Details of the disease

Dengue is the most important mosquito-borne viral infection in the world. It is a very common infection in the tropics. It causes large epidemics in these countries which include Mauritius. Dengue has a wide spectrum of outcomes that range from asymptomatic infection to death. It has a high morbidity and relatively low mortality.

Epidemiology of Dengue

The incident of dengue has grown dramatically around the world in recent decades. The actual numbers of dengue cases are under underreported and many cases are misclassified. A one recent estimate indicates 390 million dengue infections per year (95% credible interval 284-528 million), of which 96 million (67-136 million) manifest clinically (with any severity of disease). A study, of the prevalence of dengue, estimates that 3.9 billion people, in 128 countries, are at risk of infection with dengue viruses.

Transmission

Dengue virus is primarily transmitted by Aedes mosquitos, particularly A.aegypt. These mosquitos usually live between the latitudes of 35 North and 35 South below an elevation of 1000 metres (3,300 ft). They typically bite during the early morning and in the evening, but they may bite and thus spread infection at any time of day. Other Aedes species that transmit the disease include A. albopictus. Humans are the primarily host of the virus, but it also circulates in nonhuman primates. Dengue can also be transmitted via infected blood products and through organ donation.

Dengue Fever in Mauritius- 2019

The Ministry of Health has identified 24 cases of dengue fever since the beginning of the year, mainly in the Vallee des Pretres region. While less than four cases of dengue fever are idenified each year, the health authorities are already pulling the alarm that we might be facing an epidemic in the island.

The number of people affected by the dengue fever has been increasing since the beginning of the year. In fact, the Ministry of Health has identified 26 cases of dengue fever up-to-date, among which 21 people who are affected come from the Vallee des Pretres region and the rest from Coromandel, Richelieu and Rose Hill and Quatre Bornes regions.

Symptoms & Signs

The Principal symptoms of dengue are:

Severe Headache
Severe Eye Pain
Joint Pain
Muscle or Bone Pain
Rash
Nose or Gum Bleeding
Severe abdominal Pain
Drowsiness
Pale Skin
Difficulty Breathing

Generally, younger children and those with their first dengue infection have a milder illness than older children and adults.

Watch for warning signs as temperature decline 3 to 7 days after symptoms began

In case you think you have the corresponding Dengue Symptoms, go immediately to the colsest health care provider.

Life Cycle of a Mosquito

Mosquitoes undergo complete changes; they go through four distinct stages of develpment during a lifetime. the four stages are egg,pupa,larva and adult

Breeding of Mosquitoes occurs mainly in stagnemt water

Potential Breeding Sites

1. stagnant water in tyres

Prevention

Fill used tyre with soil

2. Blocked Roof Gutter

Prevention

Keep Gutter clean to allow flow of water

3. stagnant water in flower plate

Prevention

Remove water from flower plate

4. Waste in the surrounding

Prevention

Keep the environment clean
